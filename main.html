Architectural Wonders

Gothic Architecture

Gothic architecture is a style that flourished in Europe during the High and Late Middle Ages. It evolved from Romanesque architecture and was succeeded by Renaissance architecture. Characteristic features include pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses.

Modern Architecture

Modern architecture emerged in the first half of the 20th century and became dominant after World War II. It was based upon new technologies of construction, particularly the use of glass, steel, and reinforced concrete. The style emphasizes form following function, simplicity, and a rejection of ornament.
